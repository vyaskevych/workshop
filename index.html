Workshop on Cybersecurity Providing in Information and Telecommunication Systems

CPITS 2024

February 28, 2024 Kyiv, Ukraine

The aim of Cybersecurity Providing in Information and Telecommunication Systems (CPITS) Workshop is to provide a forum for researchers to discuss current trends in cybersecurity and risk management, critical infrastructure security, wireless and sensor network privacy, IoT and IoMT security, routing techniques for cybersecurity, cybersecurity data science, artificial intelligence, machine learning in cybersecurity, deepfake attacks and cryptography. In CPITS, we encourage the submission of papers in the field of Cybersecurity. Novel applications of these methods to real-world problems are welcome.

Accepted papers that will be presented at the workshop shall be submitted to CEUR-WS.org which sponsors our workshop with an electronic platform for posting materials (eISSN: 1613-0073).

This workshop is held in honor of the blessed memory of Professor Volodymyr Buriachok.

CPITS will be indexed in Scopus, DBLP, etc.

Topics

Wholeheartedly all the researchers who work in all areas of CPITS to take part in the workshop and publish the research results relevant to such main topics:

Cybersecurity and Cryptography Machine Learning and Telecommunications in Cybersecurity Wireless Network Privacy Risk Management and Economic Security Cybersecurity and Computer Science Education

Call for Contribution

We invite academic researchers and practitioners to share their work by giving a talk or presenting a poster in pure English. Contributions should be submitted through EasyChair in two different categories:

  1. A short paper (with references) but has between 5-9 CEUR-WS pages.
  2. A regular paper has at least 10 CEUR-WS pages and an appropriate number of references.

When designing your сontribution, use the new Word Template in CEURART for Two Column. Papers designed according to other templates will be rejected (and see an example).

When preparing materials, pay attention to the restrictions:

  1. One author can submit up to two сontributions.
  2. The number of authors in one сontribution should be no more than five.
  3. Self-citation should not exceed 30% (of the total number of sources).

Contributions will be reviewed and selected based on relevance and quality. Contributors will have the option to indicate their preference for the format of the presentation, but organizers reserve the right to the final decision in the matter.
